drawKISS

Keep It Simple, Sketcher

Why I Built This

I'm a developer and traditional artist based in Mauritius. I work with pencils (5H to 14B) and Indian ink, creating urban sketches, botanical illustrations, and visual stories about my island home.

Being color blind, I've embraced achromatic art—focusing on light, form, and the texture that only hand-drawn cross-hatching can provide.

The Problem

My old workflow was painfully slow: Take a photo on my phone. Email it to myself. Download it on my laptop. Open Affinity. Add a grid layer. Convert to black and white. View it fullscreen next to my easel. Grid my paper. Then finally... draw.

All that friction was killing my momentum. I wanted to draw more, not manage files.

The Solution

drawKISS automates the boring parts. Snap a photo, apply filters (edges, posterize, threshold), overlay a grid, and start sketching immediately. The reference appears on my Mac screen, gridded and processed, ready for my easel.

Tech + Art in 2026

As AI-generated imagery becomes ubiquitous, the "imperfections" of hand-drawn art are becoming a premium luxury. The texture of paper, the weight of a 14B pencil, the visible cross-hatching—these are proof of human presence.

This tool helps me get to the human part faster. Less tech friction, more ink on paper.
