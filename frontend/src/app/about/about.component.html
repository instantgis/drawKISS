Keep It Simple, Sketcher

Why I Built This

I'm Alain d'Espaignet, a developer and a Dodo with a natural ability to draw, based in Blue Bay, Mauritius. I work with pencils (5H to 14B Corinne sent me), charcoal and Indian ink, creating urban sketches, botanical illustrations, and visual stories about my island home.

Being color blind, I've embraced achromatic artâ€”focusing on light, form, and the texture that only hand-drawn cross-hatching can provide.

The Problem

My old workflow was painfully slow:

  • Roam around Dodo land
  • Spot a subject
  • Take a photo on my phone
  • Email it to myself
  • Download it on my laptop
  • Open Affinity
  • Add a grid layer
  • Convert to black and white
  • View it fullscreen next to my easel
  • Grid my paper
  • Then finally... draw

All that friction was killing my momentum. I wanted to draw more, not manage files.

The Solution

drawKISS automates the boring parts:

  • Snap a photo
  • Apply filters (edges, posterize, threshold)
  • Overlay a grid
  • Start sketching immediately

The reference appears on my MacBook Air attached to my easel, gridded and processed, ready to draw.
